Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Voyager Map mobile application (the “App”) processes personal data. The App provides travel-related features such as discovering points of interest, saving destinations, map interaction, guest access, and related communication features.

This Policy also applies to the Voyager Map website, including cookie-based analytics and consent management used on the website.

Important: This Policy distinguishes between (a) features and processing that are currently active and (b) features that are planned or conditionally enabled in the future. Where a processing activity is not currently active, it is marked as planned / where applicable.

1. Data Controller

The App is operated by an individual developer:

  • Developer: Voyager Map (individual developer)
  • Contact email: voyagemapsolution@gmail.com

2. Categories of Personal Data

2/A. Currently active categories

  • Account and profile data: name, email address, profile image URL (via social login), social login identifiers (Facebook, Google, Apple), authentication metadata.
  • Optional profile details: phone number, address data, date of birth, gender.
  • Location data (with permission): device location for map and nearby features, including one-time or active tracking while a feature is enabled.
  • Map and preference data: saved preferences and app interaction data needed for core functionality.
  • User-generated content: custom POIs, uploaded images or files, visibility depending on your sharing and privacy settings.
  • Guest-mode data (where applicable): guest identifiers, device and technical data, temporary preferences and content.
  • Feedback and bug reports: submitted feedback and diagnostic information.
  • Analytics and technical data: in-app events, device and app data, IP address, timestamps, SDK-generated identifiers.

2/B. Planned or conditionally enabled categories (where applicable)

  • Travel planning and itinerary data: saved plans, notes, and related metadata.
  • Trip cost data: expense entries and cost-related information provided by you.
  • Sharing and collaboration data: shared trip content, permissions, and activity data.
  • Advertising and attribution data (where applicable): identifiers and event data processed subject to legal requirements.
  • Billing and transaction data (where applicable): records required for invoicing and accounting.

We may occasionally contact you via email in connection with the operation of the App, including important service-related information, security notices, feature updates, or changes affecting your use of the App. Marketing or promotional emails are sent only where permitted by law and, where required, based on your consent. 5. PostHog Analytics and Product Tools

The App uses PostHog to understand product usage and improve reliability and user experience. Depending on configuration, PostHog may be used for analytics, feature flags, surveys, and limited session replay functionality. Reasonable measures are applied to reduce the likelihood of capturing sensitive information in analytics data or recordings.

6. Advertising and Measurement Tools

The App may use Google, Meta, and TikTok tools for analytics, attribution, and advertising measurement, subject to applicable legal requirements and user consent where required.

6/A. Website Cookies and Consent (Cookiebot + Google Analytics 4)

The Voyager Map website uses Cookiebot as a Consent Management Platform (CMP) to collect, store, and manage cookie preferences.

  • Strictly necessary cookies: used for essential website operation and security.
  • Statistics cookies: include Google Analytics 4 (GA4) for aggregate usage analytics.
  • Marketing cookies: used only if enabled and only with consent where required.

On the website, GA4 is configured to load only after the user grants consent for statistics cookies. If consent is refused, GA4 does not load and no GA4 analytics events are sent.

Legal basis for non-essential website cookies (including GA4): consent. Users can withdraw or change consent at any time via the cookie settings interface.

Where Google services involve transfers outside the EEA, such transfers are handled under applicable safeguards (for example, Standard Contractual Clauses, where applicable).

6/B. Cookie Retention (Website)

Cookie lifetimes vary by cookie type and provider. Detailed, up-to-date cookie names, purposes, providers, and retention periods are available in the Cookiebot cookie declaration presented on the website.

7. Server Logs and Diagnostics

Server logs may record technical data such as request timestamps, IP address, user-agent, and error information for operational and security purposes.
